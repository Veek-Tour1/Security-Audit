# Nigeria-Specific Threat Landscape

## Common Attack Vectors in Nigerian Public Sector

### Social Engineering
- ** impersonation**: Attackers posing as senior government officials
- **Phishing**: Targeting civil servants with fake procurement opportunities
- **Bribery**: Insider threats due to economic pressures

### Technical Threats
- **Ransomware**: Targeting poorly protected government databases
- **Data Theft**: Land records and citizen data being sold on dark web
- **System Compromise**: Weak authentication allowing unauthorized access

### Physical Security Concerns
- **Document Theft**: Physical land records being stolen for fraud
- **Equipment Theft**: Computers and servers in inadequately secured facilities
- **Unauthorized Access**: Shared office spaces with poor access control

## Local Incident Response Considerations

### Communication Protocols
- **Internal**: Must follow civil service communication channels
- **External**: Coordination with Nigeria Computer Emergency Response Team (ngCERT)
- **Public Disclosure**: Must align with government public relations guidelines

### Legal Requirements
- **NDPR Breach Notification**: 72-hour reporting requirement to NITDA
- **Internal Reporting**: Escalation to Permanent Secretary and Head of Service
- **Law Enforcement**: Involvement of Nigeria Police Force Cyber Crime Unit
